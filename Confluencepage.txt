What HR Will Evaluate
HR is NOT testing coding.
They are checking:
Are you stable?
Are you serious?
Can they afford you?
Any risk in hiring you?
🔥 2. Most Expected Questions (With How You Should Answer)
❓ Tell me about yourself
👉 Keep it short + structured (2 mins max)
Say:
I’m Krishna, a Senior Software Engineer with 10+ years of experience in backend development, primarily using Python, FastAPI, and Flask.
Currently working at Synechron, where I’ve been leading development and handling production systems end-to-end for a risk management platform.
I’ve worked extensively on API design, debugging production issues, and building scalable backend solutions.
Recently, I’ve also been exploring GenAI integrations.
Now I’m looking for opportunities where I can work on more scalable systems and grow further.
❓ Why are you looking for a change?
👉 VERY IMPORTANT — don’t be negative
Say:
I’ve been working on the same project for several years and have gained strong ownership and stability experience.
Now I’m looking for new challenges, better growth opportunities, and exposure to larger-scale systems.
❓ Current CTC?
👉 Say confidently:
My current CTC is around 31 LPA fixed, and around 33 LPA including variable.
❓ Expected CTC?
👉 Don’t say a single number
Say:
I’m looking in the range of 40 to 45 LPA, depending on the role and overall compensation structure.
❓ Notice Period?
👉 Very important (this is risk area)
Say:
Officially it’s 90 days, but it is negotiable, and I can try to bring it down to around 30 days.
❓ Any offers?
👉 If none:
Currently I’m in discussions with a few companies.
❓ Why should we hire you?
Say:
I bring strong backend expertise along with real production experience, ownership, and problem-solving skills.
I’ve handled end-to-end systems and can contribute independently from day one.
⚠️ 3. Things You MUST Avoid
❌ Don’t say:
“No growth here”
“Unfair treatment”
“Manager issues”
“Frustrated”
👉 HR will mark you as risky
💰 4. Salary Negotiation Strategy
If they push:
👉 Don’t drop immediately
If they say: “Can you come down?”
Say:
I’m open to discussion depending on the role and responsibilities, but I would prefer something close to my expected range.
🧠 5. Questions YOU Should Ask HR
Ask 2–3 questions (important)
✅ Good Questions
👉 Role clarity:
Can you explain the role and responsibilities in more detail?
👉 Client round:
What should I expect in the upcoming client rounds?
👉 Team:
Is this position for a new project or an existing team?
👉 Work model:
Is it remote, hybrid, or office-based?
👉 Notice:
Are you comfortable with my notice period?
🔥 6. Bonus Tip (Very Important)
Krishna…
👉 Speak calm + confident + not desperate
Even if you need job:
❌ Don’t show urgency
✅ Show stability
